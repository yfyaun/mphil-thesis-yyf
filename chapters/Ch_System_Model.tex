\chapter{System Model and Problem Formulation}
\label{ch:system-model}

This chapter establishes the system abstraction for distributed multimodal
sensing-assisted beam management in a vehicular network.  It distinguishes the
physical vehicle observed by the sensing system from the vehicle user equipment
(VUE) addressed by the communication system, and specifies how the two domains
are coupled through communication measurements.  The presentation proceeds from
the local network architecture and sensing observations to an NR-oriented beam
management procedure, before formulating the coupled perception, association,
and communication objectives.  The learning and inference mechanisms that
realise this model are developed in Chapter~\ref{ch:methodology}.

\section{Network Architecture}
\label{sec:system-network}

Consider a roadside vehicular network with a set of base stations (BSs)
\(\mathcal{B}\).  The formulation is centred on a target BS \(b\in\mathcal{B}\),
which serves the VUEs in its local service region and executes the corresponding
beam-management decisions.  A set \(\mathcal{N}_b\) of neighbouring distributed
sensing nodes (SNs) provides additional camera views of the same road region.
The SNs are sensing-only collaborators: they neither schedule VUEs nor establish
independent service cells in the model.  Thus, all communication measurements,
beam decisions, and association states discussed below are local to the target
BS.

Let \(\mathcal{U}_b[n]\) denote the active VUE set of BS \(b\) at sensing epoch
\(n\).  A member \(u\in\mathcal{U}_b[n]\) has a communication-side identifier
used for synchronisation, measurement reporting, scheduling, and downlink
transmission.  In contrast, the sensing system produces unlabelled physical
targets.  A vehicle that is visible to the sensors is therefore not automatically
known to be a particular VUE, and a VUE can be temporarily unobserved because of
occlusion or a sensing miss.  This distinction makes target-to-user association
an explicit part of the system model rather than an implicit consequence of
detection.

The index \(n\) refers to a sensing epoch, at which observations from the BS and
its SNs are aligned to a common local bird's-eye-view (BEV) frame.  The frame is
anchored at the target BS and covers its local service region.  A VUE entering a
new serving cell establishes a new local association there; no global identity
is assumed to persist across BSs.  The relation between sensing epochs and the
radio timing used for beam management is specified in
Section~\ref{subsec:system-timing}.

\section{Multimodal and Distributed Sensing Model}
\label{sec:system-sensing}

At sensing epoch \(n\), the observations available to BS \(b\) combine multiview
images from the BS and its neighbouring SNs with an ISAC-derived point cloud
available locally at the BS.  We write
\begin{equation}
    \mathcal{O}_{b,n}
    = \bigl\{\mathcal{I}_{b,n},\mathcal{I}_{\mathcal{N}_b,n},
    \mathcal{P}_{b,n}\bigr\},
    \label{eq:system-sensing-observation}
\end{equation}
where the three terms respectively denote the target-BS camera observations,
neighbouring-SN camera observations, and the BS-local point cloud.  Sensor
calibration maps every modality into the BEV frame of BS \(b\).  This common
representation permits complementary viewpoints to be used for vehicle
observation, while retaining a BS-centred interpretation of the beam labels.

\subsection{Camera Observations}
\label{subsec:system-camera}

Let \(\mathcal{V}_j\) be the set of cameras mounted at roadside node \(j\).  The
camera observations of the target BS and its collaborating SNs are respectively
written as
\begin{align}
    \mathcal{I}_{b,n} & = \{I_{b,v,n}: v\in\mathcal{V}_b\}, \\
    \mathcal{I}_{\mathcal{N}_b,n} & =
    \{I_{s,v,n}:s\in\mathcal{N}_b,\ v\in\mathcal{V}_s\}.
    \label{eq:system-camera-observations}
\end{align}
Each image is accompanied by fixed camera intrinsics and an extrinsic transform
to the target-BS coordinate system.  The distributed views are not treated as
separate predictions that are combined only after detection.  Instead, they
provide observations of a shared physical scene before the joint detection and
beam-prediction task.  In particular, an SN view can retain evidence of a
vehicle whose target-BS view is partly occluded, whereas the target-BS view
maintains the direct geometric relation to the serving link.

\subsection{ISAC Point-Cloud Model}
\label{subsec:system-isac}

The BS-local ISAC observation is represented as a point cloud
\begin{equation}
    \mathcal{P}_{b,n}=
    \{(\mathbf{q}_{b,m,n},a_{b,m,n})\}_{m=1}^{N^{\mathrm{pc}}_{b,n}},
    \label{eq:system-point-cloud}
\end{equation}
where \(\mathbf{q}_{b,m,n}\) is the position of a sensing point in the local
frame, \(a_{b,m,n}\) denotes optional point attributes, and
\(N^{\mathrm{pc}}_{b,n}\) is the number of retained points.  Its physical origin
can be described by the monostatic echo observation
\begin{equation}
    \mathbf{Y}^{\mathrm{echo}}_{b,n}
    = \mathbf{H}^{\mathrm{echo}}_{b,n}\mathbf{S}_{b,n}
    +\mathbf{N}_{b,n},\qquad
    \mathbf{H}^{\mathrm{echo}}_{b,n}
    =\mathbf{H}^{\mathrm{clu}}_{b,n}
    +\sum_{q\in\mathcal{Q}^{\mathrm{tar}}_{b,n}}
    \mathbf{H}^{\mathrm{tar}}_{b,q,n},
    \label{eq:system-isac-echo}
\end{equation}
where \(\mathbf{S}_{b,n}\) is the transmitted sensing waveform,
\(\mathbf{N}_{b,n}\) is receiver noise, \(\mathbf{H}^{\mathrm{clu}}_{b,n}\)
collects static clutter, and \(\mathcal{Q}^{\mathrm{tar}}_{b,n}\) indexes
target-related multipath components.  With an echo-channel estimate
\(\widehat{\mathbf{H}}^{\mathrm{echo}}_{b,n}\), obtained for example by
correlating the received echo with the known waveform, static-clutter
suppression and point extraction are abstracted as
\begin{equation}
    \widehat{\mathbf{H}}^{\mathrm{echo}}_{b,n}
    =\mathbf{Y}^{\mathrm{echo}}_{b,n}\mathbf{S}^{\dagger}_{b,n},\qquad
    \widetilde{\mathbf{H}}_{b,n}
    =\widehat{\mathbf{H}}^{\mathrm{echo}}_{b,n}
    -\widehat{\mathbf{H}}^{\mathrm{clu}}_{b,n},\qquad
    \mathcal{P}_{b,n}=
    \mathcal{E}_{\mathrm{pc}}\bigl(\widetilde{\mathbf{H}}_{b,n}\bigr).
    \label{eq:system-isac-extraction}
\end{equation}
Equation~\eqref{eq:system-isac-extraction} is an observation abstraction: it
explains how target-related propagation can give rise to a geometric point
cloud, without claiming a complete ISAC waveform or radar signal-processing
implementation.  The point-cloud data used in the evaluation are treated as the
available output of this sensing stage.

\section{Communication and Beam Management}
\label{sec:system-communication}

The communication model captures the measurement and decision interfaces needed
to assess sensing-assisted beam management.  It follows the main separation in
5G NR beam management between coarse synchronisation using synchronisation
signal blocks (SSBs), refinement using channel-state-information reference
signals (CSI-RSs), and downlink transmission based on the resulting channel
information.  It is an NR-oriented abstraction of these functions, rather than
a full protocol-stack model.

\subsection{Timing and Measurements}
\label{subsec:system-timing}

The radio-frame duration is \(T_{\mathrm{frame}}=10\,\mathrm{ms}\).  For
subcarrier spacing numerology \(\mu\), one slot occupies
\begin{equation}
    T_{\mathrm{slot}}=\frac{1}{2^{\mu}}\,\mathrm{ms}.
    \label{eq:system-slot-duration}
\end{equation}
The sensing update interval \(T_{\mathrm{sen}}\), SSB periodicity
\(T_{\mathrm{SSB}}\), and CSI-RS periodicity \(T_{\mathrm{CSI}}\) need not be
identical.  Each sensing epoch \(n\) is associated with a radio-time interval
in which the most recent observation, the available measurements, and the
beam-management decision are used consistently.  The concrete periodicities and
resource allocations are fixed uniformly for all policies in the evaluation.

In conventional operation, an SSB sweep provides a coarse serving-beam or
serving-SSB measurement.  The BS then transmits CSI-RSs over candidate narrow
beams to refine the choice, and the VUE reports the relevant measurement or
channel information.  The selected CSI-RS beam supports effective-channel
estimation and subsequent downlink precoding.  This sequence is also used for
initial access, recovery after a serving-cell change, and reassociation after a
loss of reliable sensing evidence.

Sensing assistance changes only the CSI-RS candidate-selection step.  If a
currently observed target has been reliably associated with VUE \(u\), its
predicted beam distribution can rank the CSI-RS beams that should be measured.
If the association is unavailable or fails its reliability test, the procedure
reverts to conventional refinement.  Hence, sensing never replaces the
communication measurement that selects the final service beam.

\subsection{Beam Selection and Precoding}
\label{subsec:system-beam-precoding}

For BS \(b\), let \(\mathcal{F}^{\mathrm{SSB}}_b\) and
\(\mathcal{F}^{\mathrm{CSI}}_b\) denote the SSB and CSI-RS beam codebooks,
with \(M_{\mathrm{SSB}}=48\) and \(M_{\mathrm{CSI}}=192\) entries,
respectively.  Each CSI-RS beam \(k\in\{1,\ldots,M_{\mathrm{CSI}}\}\) has an
SSB parent given by \(\varphi_b(k)\in\{1,\ldots,M_{\mathrm{SSB}}\}\).  For a
VUE \(u\), the narrow-beam quality at epoch \(n\) is
\begin{equation}
    g_{b,u,n}(k)=
    \left|\mathbf{h}^{\mathrm{H}}_{b,u,n}
    \mathbf{f}^{\mathrm{CSI}}_{b,k}\right|^2,
    \qquad k\in\{1,\ldots,M_{\mathrm{CSI}}\},
    \label{eq:system-beam-quality}
\end{equation}
where \(\mathbf{h}_{b,u,n}\) is the downlink channel and
\(\mathbf{f}^{\mathrm{CSI}}_{b,k}\) is the corresponding beamforming vector.
The associated normalised vector is the CSI beam-quality distribution used as
the supervision target for prediction.

Three beam-list sizes play different roles.  \(K_{\mathrm{model}}\) is the
length of the distribution produced by the perception model,
\(K_{\mathrm{bind}}\) is the number of high-ranked entries inspected when
forming a target-to-user association, and \(K_{\mathrm{scan}}\) is the number
of CSI-RS beams actually measured for a beam-management decision.  In the
present formulation, \(K_{\mathrm{model}}=M_{\mathrm{CSI}}\), whereas
\(K_{\mathrm{bind}}\) and \(K_{\mathrm{scan}}\) are protocol-level choices.
Keeping them separate prevents a rich predictive output from being mistaken for
an equally large pilot budget.

Let \(\mathcal{C}_{b,u,n}\) be the CSI-RS candidate set selected for VUE \(u\).
After CSI-RS measurements \(\widetilde{g}_{b,u,n}(k)\) are obtained over this
set, the service beam is chosen as
\begin{equation}
    k^{\star}_{b,u,n}=
    \underset{k\in\mathcal{C}_{b,u,n}}{\arg\max}\;
    \widetilde{g}_{b,u,n}(k).
    \label{eq:system-service-beam}
\end{equation}
For a scheduled VUE set \(\mathcal{U}^{\mathrm{sch}}_b[n]\), let
\(\widehat{\mathbf{H}}_b[n]\) collect the effective channel estimates after
this measurement and reporting procedure.  The BS forms a regularised
zero-forcing (RZF) precoder according to
\begin{equation}
    \mathbf{W}_b[n]=\widehat{\mathbf{H}}_b^{\mathrm{H}}[n]
    \left(\widehat{\mathbf{H}}_b[n]\widehat{\mathbf{H}}_b^{\mathrm{H}}[n]
    +\lambda_b\mathbf{I}\right)^{-1}\mathbf{D}_b[n],
    \label{eq:system-rzf-precoder}
\end{equation}
where \(\lambda_b\) is the regularisation factor and \(\mathbf{D}_b[n]\)
normalises the allocated transmit power.  The system-level link model therefore
retains the effect of candidate selection on both CSI-RS overhead and the
quality of the channel information used for multi-user transmission.

\section{Problem Formulation}
\label{sec:system-problem}

The proposed system is not posed as a single monolithic optimisation because its
outputs operate at different interfaces: sensing produces target-indexed
evidence, communication measurements are VUE-indexed, and the final criterion
is user-level communication performance.  This section therefore states three
connected objectives that make these interfaces explicit.

\subsection{Sensing and Beam Prediction}
\label{subsec:system-sensing-problem}

From \(\mathcal{O}_{b,n}\), the sensing model \(\mathcal{G}_{\theta}\) produces
a set of unbound target detections and beam distributions,
\begin{equation}
    \mathcal{G}_{\theta}(\mathcal{O}_{b,n})
    \longmapsto
    \left\{\bigl(d_{b,i,n},\mathbf{p}_{b,i,n}\bigr):
    i\in\mathcal{D}_{b,n}\right\},
    \qquad
    \mathbf{p}_{b,i,n}\in[0,1]^{M_{\mathrm{CSI}}},\qquad
    \sum_{k=1}^{M_{\mathrm{CSI}}}p_{b,i,n}(k)=1.
    \label{eq:system-perception-mapping}
\end{equation}
Here, \(d_{b,i,n}\) contains the estimated vehicle extent and confidence in the
BEV frame, while \(\mathbf{p}_{b,i,n}\) estimates the energy distribution over
CSI-RS beams for that physical target.  The objective is joint vehicle detection
and conditional beam-quality prediction: a useful distribution should rank the
beams that are strong for the detected target, but it does not itself identify a
communication user or declare a service beam.  Detection quality and
beam-ranking quality consequently characterise complementary, rather than
interchangeable, aspects of the sensing output.

\subsection{Association and Tracking}
\label{subsec:system-association-problem}

Let \(\mathcal{R}_{b,n}\) denote the set of active perception tracks.  Each
track \(r\in\mathcal{R}_{b,n}\) maintains a BEV kinematic state
\(\mathbf{x}_{r,n}=[x_{r,n},y_{r,n},v_{x,r,n},v_{y,r,n}]^{\mathrm{T}}\).  The
target-to-user association is a partial mapping
\begin{equation}
    \pi_{b,n}:\mathcal{U}_b[n]\longrightarrow
    \mathcal{R}_{b,n}\cup\{\varnothing\},
    \label{eq:system-association-map}
\end{equation}
where \(\pi_{b,n}(u)=\varnothing\) means that no sensing-derived hint is
accepted for VUE \(u\).  The mapping is one-to-one over accepted matches.  It is
established or recovered at events such as initial access, serving-cell change,
or loss of association reliability, when conventional measurements provide
communication-side evidence.

Specifically, let \(s_{b,u,n}\) be the serving SSB and let
\(\kappa_{b,u,n}\) be a CSI-RS beam selected by a communication measurement
for VUE \(u\).  If track \(r\) carries the predicted distribution
\(\mathbf{p}_{r,n}\), a beam-likelihood matching cost can be written as
\begin{equation}
    c_{u,r,n}= -\frac{\log p_{r,n}\bigl(\kappa_{b,u,n}\bigr)}
    {\log M_{\mathrm{CSI}}}.
    \label{eq:system-association-cost}
\end{equation}
The pair is feasible only if
\(\varphi_b(\kappa_{b,u,n})=s_{b,u,n}\), and unmatched choices remain
available in the assignment.  The intended outcome is therefore not forced
coverage but a high probability of correct accepted associations.  An
interacting multiple model (IMM) tracker propagates and updates each track state
across sensing epochs, allowing the accepted association to be maintained
through normal vehicle motion.  The tracker preserves the link between an
established VUE and its perception track; it does not infer a VUE identifier
from sensing data alone.  Ground-truth identities are reserved for diagnostic
evaluation of this process.

\subsection{Rate and Resource Objectives}
\label{subsec:system-rate-objectives}

The association state determines whether a sensing prediction can constrain the
CSI-RS scan.  Let \(G_{u,n}\in\{0,1\}\) be a reliability gate derived only from
available evidence, including association confidence, detection or track
confidence, hint age, SSB consistency, observation history, and beam-score
concentration.  A sensing-assisted candidate set is used only for a currently
observed and reliably associated target:
\begin{equation}
    \mathcal{C}_{b,u,n}=
    \begin{cases}
        \operatorname{TopK}_{K_{\mathrm{scan}}}(\mathbf{p}_{\pi_{b,n}(u),n}),
        & G_{u,n}=1,\\
        \mathcal{C}^{\mathrm{conv}}_{b,u,n}, & G_{u,n}=0,
    \end{cases}
    \label{eq:system-candidate-policy}
\end{equation}
where \(\mathcal{C}^{\mathrm{conv}}_{b,u,n}\) is supplied by the conventional
refinement procedure.  In both cases, the measured CSI-RS quality in
Equation~\eqref{eq:system-service-beam} selects the transmitted beam.

Let \(\gamma^{\mathrm{RZF}}_{b,u,n}\) denote the post-precoding downlink SINR
obtained with Equation~\eqref{eq:system-rzf-precoder}, and let
\(\rho_{b,u,n}\) be the fraction of available resources consumed by SSBs,
CSI-RSs, control signalling, and the associated beam-management procedure.  The
overhead-adjusted effective rate is
\begin{equation}
    R^{\mathrm{eff}}_{b,u,n}=
    \bigl(1-\rho_{b,u,n}\bigr)B_b
    \log_2\bigl(1+\gamma^{\mathrm{RZF}}_{b,u,n}\bigr),
    \label{eq:system-effective-rate}
\end{equation}
where \(B_b\) is the allocated bandwidth.  The framework is assessed through a
metric vector rather than a surrogate scalar objective: perception and
beam-ranking quality, accepted-association accuracy and coverage, candidate
recall, CSI-RS overhead, effective rate, lower-tail rate, and outage.  This
evaluation structure exposes the full causal chain from distributed sensing to
the communication benefit available to the intended VUE.  The concrete learning
objectives, association procedure, and resource-accounting protocol are
specified in the following chapters.
