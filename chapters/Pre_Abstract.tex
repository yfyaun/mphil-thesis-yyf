\begin{abstract}
Vehicular networks operating at millimetre-wave and other high-frequency bands
require accurate and timely beam management, yet repeated beam sweeping can
consume substantial radio resources in dynamic road environments. This thesis
studies a multimodal sensing-assisted framework for user association and
tracking in vehicular beam management. The proposed BS-centric setting combines
multiview cameras at the serving base station, multiview cameras at distributed
sensing nodes, and a BS-side ISAC-like point cloud. These observations are
fused in a bird's-eye-view representation to support vehicle detection,
per-target beam power distribution prediction, and Top-K candidate beam
selection. To bridge sensing outputs and communication identities, the framework
uses SSB beam sweep spectra for initial target-user association and an
interacting multiple model filter for subsequent tracking-assisted beam
maintenance. A system-level communication abstraction based on beam training
overhead, beam mismatch, zero-forcing precoding, effective SINR, spectral
efficiency, and overhead-adjusted throughput is introduced to evaluate how
sensing, association, and tracking affect communication performance. The thesis
also defines a multimodal simulation and evaluation methodology that connects
road traffic generation, multisensor observations, ray-tracing-based beam
labels, and communication metrics for vehicular networks.
\end{abstract}
